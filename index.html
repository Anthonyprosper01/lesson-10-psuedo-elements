Agriculture is the practice of cultivating soil, growing crops, and raising animals for food, fiber, medicinal plants, and other products used to sustain and enhance human life. It is one of the oldest industries in the world, dating back thousands of years, and is foundational to human civilization. Over time, agriculture has evolved from simple subsistence farming to highly complex, large-scale industrial practices, thanks to advancements in technology, machinery, and agricultural sciences. This evolution has led to higher crop yields, better disease management, and more efficient resource use, significantly improving global food production and security.

The main branches of agriculture include crop production (which involves the growing of plants for food, feed, and fiber) and livestock farming (which focuses on breeding and raising animals for food, labor, and other by-products). In addition, there are specialized fields like horticulture, which deals with fruits, vegetables, and ornamental plants, and aquaculture, which involves the farming of aquatic organisms such as fish and shellfish. Agriculture also plays a critical role in the economy of many countries, especially in developing regions where it provides employment, supports rural development, and contributes to exports and trade.
